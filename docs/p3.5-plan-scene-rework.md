## Phase 3.6 OUTSTANDING — shell side-view rework (do this first)

**The complaint (user):** far-away and extreme-close-up read well, but the profile/side view "just looks like this sideways pill with grooves," and "the side might be fundamentally flawed and these are just band-aids." This is correct — it is a structural problem, not a tuning one. Stop band-aiding the shader; the fix is in the base form.

**Root-cause diagnosis.** The form's character lives in exactly ONE frequency tier — the high-frequency rope-ridge layer (`RIDGE_RELIEF ≈ 0.85` of fine coiled detail). The low-frequency **silhouette** has almost no sculpting:
- `SHAPE` + `radialTaper` + `BOTTOM_FLAT` → a smooth squashed-ellipsoid outline.
- The central groove (`valley`) is `grooveUnderFade`'d to the crown only, so from the side there is **no visible cleft at all** — the two halves never separate in the outline.
- `mound` is a small crown-only swell; `asym` (0.35) and `undulate` (0.15) are tiny.

So the side profile = plain ellipsoid outline + surface grooves = "pill with grooves." Nothing breaks the silhouette. A convincing organic multi-lobe form needs a **3-tier frequency hierarchy**, and only tier 3 exists:
1. **MACRO lobes** (silhouette-breaking) — the outline itself must bulge and cleave into distinct masses. **MISSING.**
2. **MAJOR folds** (a few deep, wide, low-frequency channels carried in the displacement) — the flanks need major folds, not uniform contour lines. **MISSING.**
3. **FINE coils** — the current rope layer. Fine as-is.

**Rework proposal (all in `baseForm`, so it stays consistent across the three shader sites automatically):**
- **Separate the two halves in the silhouette:** extend the central groove down the front and back faces (not just the crown) so the halves read as full rounded lobes from the side. Currently `grooveCrown`/`grooveUnderFade` confine it to the top — let it wrap most of the way around, fading only at the very base.
- **Distinct lower-rear lobe mass** (the user's "cerebellum" request, in neutral terms: a smaller offset lobe cluster below-and-behind the main dome): a broad, LARGE-amplitude `pow(max(dot(dir, LOWER_REAR_DIR), 0), k)` bulge — a real silhouette feature, unlike the old small foot-lobes that were removed — ideally carrying its own few tighter parallel folds so it reads as its own textured region.
- **3–5 major sulci (tier 2):** large, deep, smooth low-frequency channels (explicit directions or low-freq noise), carried in the DISPLACEMENT so they break the silhouette, distinct from the fine ropes.
- **Front↔back asymmetry:** bias `radialTaper`/`SHAPE` along Z so one end tapers/narrows and the other is broader/blunter — the current long axis is too symmetric.

**Constraints for whoever does it:**
- New deep channels MUST use bounded-slope wall profiles (smoothstep / `pow>1` shoulder-round, like the current `valley` at `shell-shape.glsl` ~L69) or they re-introduce the triangle-shredding the 3.6 resolution rework just fixed. Macro lobes/sulci are low-frequency so they don't stress the Nyquist clamp — but the clamp guards the *rope* layer, not these; keep channel walls smooth.
- Preserve the shipped conventions the camera track depends on: **long axis = Z, groove in the X=0 plane, crown = +Y.** Do not rotate/rescale the overall axes; the timing-locked keyframes assume them.
- Review via `tissue-preview.html?ry=90` (pure side) and `?rx=30&ry=-35` (3/4) at **dpr 2** — the SILHOUETTE is the acceptance test. Validate the side read BEFORE touching the camera.
- Camera keyframes and breakthrough timing are user-locked; the resculpt must not require moving them.

## Verified load-bearing facts

- The cleft is keyed purely on `|dir.x|` (`shell-shape.glsl:46,54`) — a great circle in the X=0 plane. Re-orienting the anisotropic scale (long axis → Z, flatten Y) makes the *same* formula read as front-to-back over the top. The resculpt is a rescale + notch/lobe removal, **not** a geometry rewrite.
- The meridian stripe axis is baked into `atan(dir.z, |dir.x|)` evaluated in **three places** that must stay consistent: `ridgeProfile` (`shell-shape.glsl:113`), `detailHeight` (`:136`), and the frag AA phase (`tissue-shell.frag.glsl:48`).
- The dissolve aperture is hard-coded to world +z on **two** axes: `front = smoothstep(-0.35, 0.7, normalize(vWorldPos).z)` AND `radial = length(vWorldPos.xy)` (`tissue-shell.frag.glsl:152-153`). Both must follow the authored approach axis.
- RD texture is sampled as a flat planar projection `vWorldPos.xy*0.04+0.5` (`:142`) — smears the moment the camera sees the flanks. Needs a dir-based equirect UV.
- The shell's self-fog uniforms (`uFogColor/uFogDensity`) are set once and never updated — invisible today (density constant), visibly wrong once fog density becomes depth-driven. Fix required, not optional.
- Per-section scroll runway is already freely variable: `rawProgressToDepth` remaps arbitrary DOM heights onto fixed canonical bands, with ResizeObserver + fonts.ready re-measure. Only `ScaleSection`'s `full` (pinned 100vh) needs changing.
- No intro infrastructure exists anywhere (no component, no store flag, no scroll lock). `DepthIndicator` has live keyboard/click scale-jump nav that must be guarded during the overture.
- `content/sections/brain.md` has an unused `subtitle` frontmatter field — the arrival block's one-liner, already authored.
- `frameloop="demand"`: any new animation (intro timeline, parallax spring, mote drift) must call `invalidate()` or ride the ambient ticker.

### WP1 — Resculpt + ridge-field rework (shader-only; parallel with WP0; reviewed via tissue-preview at dpr 2)
**Base form** (`shell-shape.glsl`, `tissue-shell.vert.glsl`):
- `SHAPE`: elongate Z ~1.35× (long axis), flatten Y ~0.8×, X near baseline → horizontal loaf; the existing cleft now reads front-to-back for free.

**Ridge field — consolidate the three `atan` sites into one:**
- `ridgeFlow` returns `vec3(theta, warp, branch)`; `theta` becomes a blended flow angle: groove-parallel near the cleft → lateral wrap on the flanks, blended **as unit vectors** (never raw angles — ±π wrap seam), with `warp` added to theta so wandering/looping happens on the blended direction. `ridgeProfile`/`detailHeight`/frag AA all consume `flow.x` and delete their own `atan` calls. No new snoise/fbm calls (stays in the ~16-call/vertex budget); meander amplitude and `RIDGE_N` are eye-tuned.
**RD promotion (fragment-only — never in the displacement path):**
- Sample UV → dir-based equirect (`atan(dir.z,dir.x)`, `dir.y`), damped by existing `poleCapFade` to hide polar pinch.
- Thread `rd` into `detailHeight`'s fine-layer amplitude/crest mask (locally-constant across FD taps, like `finePhase`) for micro-relief; strengthen albedo modulation ±5% → ~±12–15% via `uRDBlend`. `ridgeProfile` untouched by RD (shared with vertex displacement; must stay stable during RD warmup). `uLook` stays 0 (crisp).
