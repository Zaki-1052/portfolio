description: Epigenetics research at Ferguson Lab

My two main projects at the Ferguson Lab focused on the effects of knocking out BAP1 -- an important tumor suppressor gene -- in mice, and measuring how both the 3D folding of the genome, along with DNA methylation, were reshaped due to the histone modification H2AK119ub. I characterized dysregulation of chromatin loops in euchromatin and facultative heterochromatin; as well as gene repression at neuronal and synaptic loci. 

By intersecting chromatin state with the pathology of our mouse models, I was able to present at a conference and symposium, and author two papers based on my work classifying an invasion of Polycomb collapsing developmental long-range contacts, and further implying a previously unstudied link to methylation in the developing cerebellum.
